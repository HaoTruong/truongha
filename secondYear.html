First Ever Co-op

The most impact experience for me this year is working as an intern for my first co-op, I am lucky enough to land a job as quality assurance for Fox Sports Cincinnati. Even though that I was not my dream job, and I was not satisfied with what I have achieved for my first co-op experience, it is still the best thing that happen to me in this year. I always imagine myself working as back-end software developer who will spend all day reading and writing codes, so landing the quality assurance job was not the best ideal job I could get, I mostly spent my day testing website making it run perfectly. But luckily I met a great supervisor who taught me a lot about communication in professional workplace, and because as a first year intern, I have a lot of free time after finishing my work, my supervisor let me work on a small portion of back-end development work so I can experience the work that I really want to do. When the Covid-19 pandemic hit Ohio, I heard some students had to end the co-op semester early because of it, but we was lucky enough to be given a change to work from home, and that was the first time I experienced the difficulty of working from home. I am always ready to do any work in the office but when I started working from home, there were so many distractions around me, starting some work got harder and harder, that is when I realized I need to come up with solution to disconnect myself from distractions. I started separate my work and home computer to make sure when I work, there will be no application or website that I need to open when I use my home computer, that helped me concentrate on what I need to be done. I met a lot of friends who were also co-op students as me, and I learned a lot from them, from how they work, how they communicate with each other. I met those who were upperclassmen in my major and learned a lot about classes I would take for summer semester, and they also helped me know more about my dream job. Even though not being able to land my dream job, I got a chance to experience another piece in my tech world and worked with amazing people from my workplace. I love working as back-end developer but always doubt myself if I really enjoy it when I work on it, and having an opportunity to have a small work on it at work was great to assure I will enjoy working on it.
